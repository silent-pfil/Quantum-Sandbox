# Quantum Sandbox
A visual learning tool for quantum mechanics. Built by someone who couldn't learn from textbooks

# What this is
I struggled with chemistry and physics in school. Reading and drawing diagrams did nothing for me. I always believed that if I could see atoms in 3D, actually fly around them and watch them behave, I would have learned faster and remembered more.
Universe Sandbox does this for the cosmos. I asked Claude (Anthropic's AI) to help me build something similar at the quantum scale. This is the result.
I have no formal education in physics. Just curiosity, stubbornness, and access to good tools.

# What it does
All 118 elements via a full periodic table selector
Electron probability clouds sampled from real quantum wave functions, not decorative spheres
Orbital shapes: s (spherical), p (dumbbell), d (cloverleaf), f (multi-lobe) — each reflecting actual angular momentum states
Quantum flicker — particles continuously jump to new valid positions, representing the discontinuous nature of quantum transitions
Nucleus scaled by the nuclear radius formula r = r₀A^(1/3), with quark substructure visible in light elements
A guided tour for first-time visitors
A live scale bar calibrated to real atomic units (Ångströms / picometers)
Single HTML file — no install, no app, just open in a browser


# What I know is approximate
The nucleus is shown roughly 10,000× larger than physical scale relative to the electron cloud. True scale would make one or the other invisible
Neutron counts use a simplified isotope approximation
Multi-electron orbital shapes are shown as independent subshells; in reality electron-electron interactions deform them
Heavy element (Z > 83) configurations are based on the Aufbau principle with known exceptions noted. Superheavy elements (Z > 104) are theoretical


# The roadmap
This is a Tier 1 and 2 prototype. The full vision:

Tier 3 — Particle collider simulator with simplified but scientifically honest Monte Carlo collision outcomes
Tier 4 — Full quantum sandbox: place atoms, watch molecules form. H + O + O → water. The geometry emerges from the physics.
